## Sales Strategy Notes

### Priority Approach
1. **Tier 1 (Large chains):** Target corporate/HQ procurement. A single deal = hundreds of locations. KidsFoundation (#1) and Partou (#2) alone cover ~50,000+ child places. Offer pilot program at 2-3 flagship locations.
2. **Tier 2 (Mid-size):** Regional decision-makers. Faster sales cycle. Offer on-site demo.
3. **Tier 3 (Premium/Innovative):** Easiest to convert â€” these organizations actively seek differentiating experiences. Use as reference cases for Tier 1 & 2.

### Key Selling Points for Dutch Market
- **Quality differentiation** in a competitive market with waitlists
- **Sensory/motor development** alignment with Dutch pedagogical standards
- **Space efficiency** â€” interactive floor/wall projections don't require additional physical equipment
- **Weather-independent indoor play** (important in Dutch climate)
- **Parent appeal** â€” differentiator when parents are choosing between providers

### Market Context
- ~672,000 child places across ~17,365 locations in NL (2024 data)
- High consolidation ongoing (M&A activity, e.g. KidsFoundation + Partou merger announced)
- Government subsidies support childcare investment
- Staff shortages make technology-assisted play attractive
- BMK (Branchevereniging Maatschappelijke Kinderopvang) membership = quality signal; 66% of top 50 are BMK members

### Recommended First Moves
1. Book demo with **Kindergarden** (premium, high revenue, innovation-friendly)
2. Approach **Casa Bilingual Montessori** or **Treehouse Montessori** for pilot/case study
3. Submit to **KidsFoundation** and **Partou** corporate procurement with case study from pilot
4. Attend **Kinderopvang Totaal** trade fair for networking with mid-size operators
