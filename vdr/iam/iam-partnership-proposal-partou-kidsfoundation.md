## 2. Problem Statement

### Children Are Moving Less
Dutch children spend increasing time on screens. The Kenniscentrum Sport & Bewegen reports that a majority of young children do not meet daily physical activity guidelines. Childcare centers ‚Äî where children spend 8‚Äì10 hours daily ‚Äî are a critical intervention point.

### Differentiation Pressure
The Dutch childcare market is competitive. With ~9,315 kinderdagverblijven nationwide, parents compare locations on quality, innovation, and engagement. Traditional play equipment wears out, requires constant replacement (‚Ç¨500‚Äì‚Ç¨1,500/year), and offers limited variety.

### Pedagogical Demands
GGD inspections and VVE (Voorschoolse en Vroegschoolse Educatie) programs require documented developmental activities. Staff spend significant time preparing activities and managing transitions ‚Äî time that could be better supported by smart tools.

### The Opportunity for Large Chains
Partou and KidsFoundation, as market leaders, are uniquely positioned to set the standard for innovation in Dutch childcare. A technology partnership at scale creates competitive moats that independent operators cannot easily replicate.

## 4. Partnership Structure

We propose a phased approach that minimizes risk and maximizes learning at each stage.

### Phase 1: Pilot Program (Month 1‚Äì3)
- **Scope:** 2‚Äì4 locations per chain (4‚Äì8 total)
- **Duration:** 4 weeks free trial per location, followed by 8-week paid evaluation
- **Selection criteria:** Mix of urban/suburban, different age groups, varying room sizes
- **What IAM provides:**
  - Full system installation at no cost during trial
  - Staff training (30-minute on-site introduction)
  - Daily usage tracking tools and weekly check-ins
  - Dedicated account manager
- **What we measure:**
  - Daily usage hours and child participation
  - Staff adoption and ease of use
  - Parent feedback and reactions
  - Impact on enrollment inquiries during pilot period
  - Pedagogical alignment (VE goals, GGD relevance)
- **Decision point:** End of Month 3 ‚Äî joint review of pilot data

### Phase 2: Initial Rollout (Month 4‚Äì9)
- **Scope:** 25‚Äì50 locations per chain
- **Model:** Lease agreement with volume pricing (see Section 5)
- **Rollout pace:** 8‚Äì12 installations per month

### Phase 3: Full Network Deployment (Month 10‚Äì24)
- **Scope:** Remaining locations based on suitability assessment
- **Target:** 200‚Äì400+ systems across both chains
- **Model:** Multi-year framework agreement with annual pricing reviews
