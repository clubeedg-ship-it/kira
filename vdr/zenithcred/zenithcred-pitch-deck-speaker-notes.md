## Slide 2: The Problem — Corporate Wellness Crisis

### Speaker Notes
"Let me frame the problem in three layers.

**First:** Employee engagement is at historic lows. Gallup's 2024 data shows only 21% of global employees are engaged. Worse — manager engagement dropped to 27%, the steepest decline ever. This matters because 70% of team engagement comes from the manager.

**Second:** The mental health cost is staggering. WHO estimates 12 billion working days lost annually to depression and anxiety — that's a trillion dollars in lost productivity. This isn't a 'nice to have' problem anymore; it's a P&L problem.

**Third — and this is key:** Existing solutions are failing. Wellness apps see declining usage after month one. They're just another screen, another notification. There's a fundamental disconnect between digital-only solutions and the physical workspace where people actually spend their time.

The market sees this too — growing at 7.4% CAGR to $129 billion by 2034. But the solutions haven't kept up with the problem."

### Anticipated Tough Questions

**Q: "Isn't this just a COVID-era trend? Won't engagement normalize?"**
> "Actually, the 2024 data shows it's getting worse, not better. Manager engagement hit its steepest decline in 2024 — three years post-COVID. The return-to-office movement is creating a new need: offices have to justify their existence, and wellness infrastructure is how forward-thinking companies are doing that."

**Q: "The market size seems large. Is it credible for a niche product?"**
> "Fair question. The €905M TAM is total Benelux corporate wellness. Our SAM — companies with physical offices, wellness budgets, and tech-forward cultures — is €126M. We need less than 2% of that to hit our Year 3 targets. We're not trying to capture the whole market; we're creating a new category within it."

## Slide 3: The Solution — Physical Wellness Infrastructure

### Speaker Notes
"Here's what we've built. Four components working together:

**One — Interactive Light Panels.** Not just pretty lights. 10,000 lux therapeutic lighting — that's the clinical dose for mood improvement and circadian regulation. These panels respond in real-time to team wellness data.

**Two — Gamified Team Challenges.** Points, rewards, social competition. But team-based, not individual. This is critical — team gamification drives sustained engagement because there's social accountability. Your colleague notices if you're not participating.

**Three — Biofeedback Integration.** We connect to Oura, WHOOP, Apple Health. The magic: as your team's wellness metrics improve, the office lighting literally changes. You can *see* your team getting healthier. Privacy-first — only team aggregate data is shown.

**Four — Environmental Response.** The hardware creates persistent physical presence. Unlike an app you close, the light panels are always there — ambient, always on, always reminding you that wellness matters.

The key differentiator: **we're the only solution where your office environment actively participates in your team's health.** This isn't another app. It's infrastructure."

### Anticipated Tough Questions

**Q: "Why can't Headspace or Calm just partner with a lighting company?"**
> "They could try, but the integration between gamification mechanics, biofeedback data, and real-time light response is deeply coupled. It's not 'app + lights' — it's a unified system where the hardware and software were designed together. Also, their business model is per-seat software; adding hardware complexity doesn't align with their margin structure."

**Q: "How do you handle employees who don't want to participate?"**
> "Completely opt-in. The light panels create ambient benefits for everyone — better lighting regardless. Gamification is voluntary. Team metrics are shown, individual data stays private. We've been very intentional about GDPR compliance and avoiding any 'surveillance' perception. Employee trust is our most valuable asset."

**Q: "What's the clinical evidence for light therapy in workplace settings?"**
> "Strong evidence for SAD treatment, moderate-to-strong for non-seasonal depression, and strong for circadian regulation. 10,000 lux is the established therapeutic dose. What's newer is applying this in a corporate setting with gamification — that's where our pilot data will be uniquely valuable."

## Slide 5: Business Model — Path to €1.7M ARR

### Speaker Notes
"Three revenue streams.

**SaaS subscriptions** — €12 per employee per month. This is the core — recurring, predictable, high margin at 78%.

**Hardware sales** — €5,500 average per installation. Lower margin at 25%, but it creates physical lock-in. Once those panels are on the wall, switching costs are real.

**Professional services** — installation, training, customization. Small today, meaningful at scale.

On unit economics — and I want to be transparent here because we corrected our earlier models significantly. Our blended CAC is €20,000 per customer. Conservative for enterprise B2B. Average deal size is €31,000 in year one. Three-year LTV is about €50,000, giving us a 2.5x LTV:CAC ratio.

Is 2.5x where we want to be long-term? No — we want 3x plus. But it's an honest number for a pre-revenue company. As our brand builds and churn drops, this ratio improves naturally."

### Anticipated Tough Questions

**Q: "2.5x LTV:CAC is below the 3x benchmark. Why should I invest?"**
> "Because we calculated it honestly. Most seed-stage companies claim 5x or higher using fantasy churn rates and 7-year lifetimes. We used 3-year LTV with 20-25% Year 1 churn. As we prove the product and improve retention — which hardware lock-in helps with — the path to 3x+ is clear. I'd rather under-promise and over-deliver."

**Q: "25% Year 1 churn is high."**
> "Agreed — it reflects the reality that we're still finding product-market fit. Some early customers will be wrong fits. By Year 3, we target 15%. The hardware component actually helps: once panels are installed, the physical removal barrier makes churn more considered than canceling a software subscription."

**Q: "Why not just software? Hardware is operationally complex."**
> "Hardware is our moat. Pure-software wellness is a commoditizing, crowded market. Hardware creates switching costs, persistent physical presence, and a fundamentally different user experience. Yes, it's harder — but that's exactly why no one else has done it."

## General Presentation Tips for Otto

1. **Time:** Keep the core pitch to 15 minutes. Leave 15 minutes for Q&A. Investors decide in Q&A, not the pitch.
2. **Honesty wins:** You corrected your financial model — that's a strength. Mention it briefly: "We caught errors in our original projections and corrected them. I'd rather show you honest numbers."
3. **Hardware demo:** If possible, bring a light panel to the meeting. Let them see it change colors. Physical demos are 10x more memorable than slides.
4. **The story:** Lead with "the office that cares about you." It's vivid, memorable, and differentiating.
5. **Energy:** You're asking people to believe in something that doesn't fully exist yet. Your conviction matters. Be enthusiastic but grounded.
6. **Follow up same day:** Send a thank-you email within 2 hours of every meeting with a link to the data room and specific next steps.
