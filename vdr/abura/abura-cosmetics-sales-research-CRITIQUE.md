## Executive Summary of Concerns

This report reads like an optimistic pitch deck, not a rigorous sales strategy. It contains:

- **Multiple unsourced claims** presented as fact
- **Unrealistic B2B timelines** that ignore procurement realities
- **Missing critical execution details** (budget, resources, team capacity)
- **Competitor data that may be fabricated** or outdated
- **No acknowledgment of significant barriers** to entry in recommended channels

The strategy is directionally reasonable but would fail on execution. The 6-month goals (10+ B2B partnerships, 30% wholesale revenue increase) are aspirational without evidence they're achievable for a small D2C brand with no apparent wholesale infrastructure.

## Missing Information Needed

### Critical Gaps

1. **Current financials**: What's their revenue? Profit margins? Cash runway? Can they afford wholesale margins?

2. **Team capacity**: Who will execute this? Founder-led? How many hours/week available for outreach?

3. **Existing B2B experience**: Have they tried wholesale before? Why didn't it work?

4. **Production capacity**: Can they scale if they land a hotel chain? What's their MOQ from suppliers?

5. **Regulatory compliance**: 
   - EU cosmetics regulation (CPNP notification)
   - UK SCPN post-Brexit
   - US FDA compliance for cosmetics
   - Product liability insurance

6. **Professional product sizes**: Spas need 250ml-1L. Are these planned?

7. **Payment terms capacity**: Can they handle net-30/net-60 payment terms? Cash flow implications?

8. **Budget**: Total investment available for sales expansion?

9. **Pricing validation**: Mystery shop competitors. Are the listed prices current?

10. **Customer acquisition cost**: What does it cost them to acquire a D2C customer currently?

### Questions the Report Should Have Asked

- Why are products perpetually on 50% sale?
- What's the customer retention rate?
- What's the average order value?
- How did they land the Amsterdam retail locations?
- Who is behind the brand? Connections matter for B2B.

## Suggested Fixes

### Immediate Revisions

1. **Verify all competitor data** - Mystery shop each competitor. Update prices. Confirm retail channels are current.

2. **Remove "Award-Worthy"** from templates - Either win an award or don't claim it.

3. **Right-size the timeline** - Replace "6 months to 10 partnerships" with "6 months to 2-3 partnerships + pipeline of 20."

4. **Add budget estimates** - Every recommendation needs a cost range.

5. **Create two strategy tiers**:
   - **Tier A**: Achievable with â‚¬5K and 10 hrs/week
   - **Tier B**: Requires â‚¬25K+ and dedicated sales hire

6. **Clarify pricing** - If sale price is permanent, make that the listed price. If not, address the discounting strategy.

### B2B Strategy Overhaul

1. **Start smaller**: Target independent day spas (5-10 rooms), not Soho House.

2. **Create professional sizes**: 250ml or 500ml body oils at wholesale pricing.

3. **Develop training materials**: Estheticians need protocols, ingredient education.

4. **Get product liability insurance**: Required for any serious B2B relationship.

5. **Build a case study first**: Get one spa success story before mass outreach.

6. **Consider a distributor**: Instead of direct sales, find a professional beauty distributor who already has spa relationships.

### Realistic Goal Setting

| Original Goal | Revised Goal |
|---------------|--------------|
| 10+ spa/retail partnerships in 6 months | 2-3 small spa partnerships + 1 indie retailer |
| 50+ UGC posts in 3 months | 15-20 posts from micro-influencers |
| 30% revenue from wholesale | 10% revenue from wholesale (Year 1) |
