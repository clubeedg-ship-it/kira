# CEO Daily Operating Routine Framework
**For: Otto, CEO Oopuo**  
**Workload: 10-12 hour days**  
**Updated: January 2025**

---

## 1. Morning Routine (30 minutes)
*Execute between 6:00-7:00 AM when mind is freshest*

### Immediate Priorities (First 10 minutes)
1. **Cash Position Check** - Review overnight cash balances across all entities
2. **Critical Alerts** - Check for any emergency issues from leadership team
3. **Today's P1 Decisions** - Review decisions that MUST be made today

### Key Dashboards (15 minutes)
Review in this order:
- **Real-time Cash Dashboard** - Daily cash position, burn rate alerts
- **Revenue Performance** - Yesterday's bookings, pipeline velocity changes  
- **Operational Metrics** - Key KPIs by company (customer issues, system uptime)
- **People Alerts** - Urgent HR issues, critical role status updates

### Priority Inbox (5 minutes)
- **VIP Filter**: Board members, key investors, direct reports with "URGENT" tag
- **Decision Required**: Items flagged as needing CEO decision within 24h
- **External Stakeholders**: Customer escalations, partner issues, regulatory matters

**Output Goal**: By 7:00 AM, Otto knows the current state and today's critical path.

## 2. Financial Review Cadence

### Daily (5-10 minutes, built into morning routine)
- **Cash Position**: Actual balances vs. 13-week forecast
- **Burn Rate Alerts**: Any unexpected spikes in daily spend  
- **AR Aging**: New overdue accounts (>90 days)
- **AP Urgent**: Items requiring immediate payment approval

### Weekly (30 minutes, Monday 8:00 AM)
- **P&L Flash Report** by company - actual vs. plan
- **Weekly Cash Flow**: 13-week rolling forecast updates
- **Burn Rate Analysis**: Trending and variance explanations
- **Capital Allocation**: Review upcoming investment decisions
- **Banking Relationships**: Credit facility usage, covenant compliance

### Monthly (90 minutes, 1st Friday of each month)
- **Full Financial Package**: Complete P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
- **Budget vs. Actual**: Deep dive on variances >10%
- **Forward Projections**: Update 12-month rolling forecasts  
- **Capital Structure**: Review debt positions, equity requirements
- **Board Financial Materials**: Prep for monthly board deck

**Financial Decision Authority Matrix:**
- <$10K: Direct reports can approve
- $10K-$50K: Requires Otto approval within 24h
- $50K-$250K: Requires Otto + CFO approval
- >$250K: Board committee approval required

## 6. Decision Logs & Delegation Tracking

### Decision Documentation System

#### Daily Decision Log (5 minutes at end of day)
**Template for each decision:**
```
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Decision: [Brief description]
Context: [Why this decision was needed]
Options Considered: [Brief list]
Decision Made: [What was decided]
Owner: [Who executes]
Success Metrics: [How we'll know it worked]
Review Date: [When we'll assess outcomes]
```

#### Weekly Decision Review (Friday 3:00-3:30 PM)
- **Decisions Made This Week**: Review log for completeness
- **Pending Decisions**: What needs resolution next week?
- **Decision Quality Check**: Any decisions we should revisit?
- **Delegation Effectiveness**: Are delegated items moving forward?

### Delegation Tracking Matrix

#### Level 1: Direct Report Owns (Inform Otto of outcome)
- Operational decisions within approved budget
- Hiring decisions at Director level and below  
- Customer issue resolution <$25K impact
- Process improvements within single department

#### Level 2: Direct Report Recommends (Otto approves)
- Strategic initiative resource allocation
- Budget variance >10% of department total
- Cross-functional process changes
- Partnership agreements <$100K value

#### Level 3: Otto Decides (High-stakes/high-impact)
- Strategy changes affecting multiple companies
- Leadership hiring (VP level and above)
- Investment decisions >$250K
- Board-level commitments and communications

### Delegation Follow-up Cadence
**Weekly**: Check status of all Level 2 delegated items
**Bi-weekly**: Review outcomes of Level 1 delegated decisions  
**Monthly**: Assess delegation effectiveness and adjust authority levels

### Decision Quality Metrics (Monthly Review)
- **Speed**: Average time from issue identification to decision
- **Quality**: % of decisions that achieved intended outcomes
- **Delegation**: % of decisions successfully executed by others
- **Reversal Rate**: % of decisions that needed to be changed/reversed

## Emergency Protocols

### Critical Issue Escalation
**Immediate (interrupt Otto anywhere, anytime):**
- Company-threatening legal action
- Security breach with customer data
- Key customer cancellation >$500K ARR
- Critical employee incident or death

**Within 2 Hours:**
- Product outage affecting >50% of customers
- Media crisis requiring statement
- Regulatory inquiry or audit notice
- Major competitor acquisition in our space

**Same Business Day:**
- Key partnership negotiation breakdown
- Significant budget variance discovery
- Important employee resignation
- Customer complaint escalation to CEO level

### Crisis Communication Plan
1. **Otto gets initial alert** within 15 minutes of issue identification
2. **30-minute response window** for Otto to acknowledge and assign lead
3. **2-hour update cycle** until crisis resolution
4. **Post-crisis review** within 48 hours to improve protocols
