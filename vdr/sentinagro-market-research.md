# SentinAgro — Market Research: Drone-Based Cattle Monitoring

*Compiled: February 2026 | For investor materials*

---

## Executive Summary

The convergence of precision livestock farming, agricultural drones, and AI-powered monitoring creates a significant market opportunity for SentinAgro. The global precision livestock farming market is valued at **$7–8 billion (2025)** growing at **9–11% CAGR** to **$12–18 billion by 2030–2035**. Agricultural drones are one of the fastest-growing subsegments (~20–28% CAGR). Cattle is the dominant livestock segment, and both Europe and Brazil represent high-value target regions with favorable adoption dynamics.

## 5. Regional Breakdown

### Europe
- **43% of global PLF market growth (2020–2024)** originated from Europe (Technavio)
- Leading countries: **Germany** (37.8% of European precision ag), **UK, France, Netherlands**
- Europe precision agriculture market: **$3.4B (2024)** → $6.3B by 2034 (6.5% CAGR)
- Strong regulatory push for animal welfare and sustainability
- EU Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) increasingly incentivizes precision farming
- **Key opportunity:** EU animal welfare regulations create compliance demand for monitoring

### Brazil & South America
- Brazil has the **world's largest commercial cattle herd** (~215M head)
- Agricultural drone market growing at **15.5% CAGR (2023–2028)**
- Drone usage in Brazilian agriculture surged **120%** in recent years
- Agri-drone/precision ag segment valued at **$80M** (growing rapidly)
- **Cargill CattleView** already proving drone economics: ~$6/head/year profit improvement
- Embrapa (government research) actively developing drone cattle counting with ML
- Up to **40% labor reduction** documented (University of São Paulo)
- **Key opportunity:** Vast extensive ranching operations (millions of hectares) where ground monitoring is impractical; strong government support via Embrapa

### North America
- Leads global market (~38.5% share in livestock drone management)
- US strongest market for agricultural drones
- High tech adoption but also high competition

## 6. Pain Points Drones Solve for Cattle Farmers

| Pain Point | Impact | Drone Solution |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| **Manual herd inspection** | Enormous labor cost on large ranches | Rapid aerial surveillance; minutes vs. hours |
| **Late disease detection** | Animal losses, treatment costs | Thermal imaging detects fever/stress early |
| **Inaccurate headcounts** | Financial/regulatory exposure | Aerial counting with AI; fast, accurate |
| **Lost/stolen livestock** | Direct financial loss | GPS + thermal search of large areas |
| **Pasture degradation** | Reduced carrying capacity | Aerial imagery for rotation/fertilization decisions |
| **Heat stress (tropical)** | Productivity loss, mortality | Thermal monitoring for early intervention |
| **Emergency response** | Flood/fire livestock losses | Real-time aerial assessment for relocation |
| **Labor shortages** | Industry-wide crisis | Automation reduces staffing needs 30–40% |
| **Water source monitoring** | Dehydration, disease | Aerial survey of troughs, ponds, quality |

## 7. EU Drone Regulatory Landscape (2024–2025)

### EASA Framework (EU-wide)
EU drone operations governed by Regulations (EU) 2019/945 and 2019/947, with three risk-based categories:

| Category | Risk Level | Agricultural Use | Requirements |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Open (A1/A2/A3)** | Low | Monitoring, mapping, surveying | Registration, basic pilot competency, VLOS, C-class label (mandatory from Jan 2024) |
| **Specific** | Medium | Spraying, BVLOS operations | Pre-Defined Risk Assessment (PDRA), operator declaration, national authority approval |
| **Certified** | High | Heavy payload, urban BVLOS | Full certification (rare for agriculture) |

### Key Regulatory Points
- **Remote ID** mandatory for all drones from January 1, 2024
- **C-class identification labels** (C0–C4) required on all new drones from 2024
- **Monitoring/mapping** (SentinAgro's core use case) falls in **Open category A3** — lowest regulatory burden
- Spraying requires Specific category approval (higher bar, but not SentinAgro's focus)
- **National variations exist:** Member states can grant derogations (e.g., Poland's agricultural drone derogation 2025)
- **France** enacted new drone agriculture law (April 2025) for expanded agricultural drone use
- **Spain** streamlined PDRA approval via digital platform

### Regulatory Advantage for SentinAgro
**Monitoring-only drone operations (no spraying/payload delivery) face the lowest regulatory burden in the EU.** Open category A3 allows flights in areas away from people with minimal authorization — ideal for rural cattle farms. This is a significant competitive advantage over spraying-focused drone companies that face Specific category requirements.

## 9. Key Takeaways for Investors

1. **Timing is right:** PLF market at inflection point (~$7B → $12B+ by 2030); drones are the fastest-growing technology within it
2. **No dominant player** in drone-specific cattle monitoring — greenfield opportunity
3. **Monitoring = low regulatory burden** in EU (Open category) unlike spraying competitors
4. **Brazil is the ideal launch market:** largest herd, proven economics ($6/head savings), government R&D support, 120% drone adoption growth
5. **EU expansion** offers premium pricing with CAP subsidies and welfare regulation as demand drivers
6. **SaaS model** (recurring revenue per head) is highly scalable across regions
7. **Adjacent incumbents** (Zoetis, GEA) focus on wearables/sensors — drones are a complementary, not competitive, technology
8. **AI + drone combination** creates defensible IP moat (herd counting, health detection, behavior analysis)
