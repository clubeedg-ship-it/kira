## 1. Dutch Entrepreneur Visa

### What It Is
The **"Verblijfsvergunning voor arbeid als zelfstandige"** (Residence permit for self-employed persons) ‚Äî issued by IND (Immigration and Naturalisation Service). This is NOT the startup visa; it's the general self-employment permit.

### The Points System (RVO Assessment)
The Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) evaluates applications using a **scoring system requiring minimum 90 points** across three categories:

| Category | What's Assessed | Minimum Points |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| **A: Personal Experience** | Education, work experience, entrepreneurial track record, connections to NL | 30 pts (or 45 if skipping C) |
| **B: Business Plan** | Quality, expected revenue/profit, feasibility, market analysis | 30 pts (or 45 if skipping C) |
| **C: Added Value to Dutch Economy** | Innovation, job creation, investment in patents/equipment | 30 pts |

**Two paths to qualify:**
- Score ‚â•30 in ALL three categories (total ‚â•90), OR
- Score ‚â•45 in both A and B (Category C not needed)

**‚ö†Ô∏è Rejection rate is ~90%.** This is not easy. Strong evidence is critical.

### Exemptions from Points System
- US, Japanese, or Turkish nationals (treaty exemptions)
- Long-term EU residents from other EU countries

**Otto's nationality matters here.** If he holds a Brazilian passport only ‚Äî full points system applies. If he has any treaty-country citizenship, different rules.

### Financial Requirements

| Requirement | Amount | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| **Minimum capital in Dutch business account** | ‚Ç¨4,500 | Must remain available; shown via bank statement |
| **Startup equity** | ‚Ç¨25,000 | Shown via bank statements |
| **First-year revenue projection** | ‚Ç¨50,000 | Supported by contracts, LOIs, evidence |
| **Sustainable income (for renewal)** | ~‚Ç¨1,657/month (from month 7+) | Inflation-adjusted annually |
| **Application fee** | ‚Ç¨423 | Paid within 3 weeks of application |

### Required Documents (Full Checklist)

**Identity & Personal:**
- [ ] Valid passport (‚â•6 months validity, 2 blank pages)
- [ ] Passport-size photos (Dutch standard)
- [ ] Clean criminal record certificate (apostilled/legalized)
- [ ] Proof of address

**Business:**
- [ ] KvK (Chamber of Commerce) registration ‚Äî or plan to register
- [ ] Detailed business plan addressing all points criteria
- [ ] Sales contracts / letters of intent from Dutch clients
- [ ] Partnership agreements (if any)
- [ ] Professional permits/licenses (if required for the activity)

**Financial:**
- [ ] Bank statements showing ‚Ç¨4,500 minimum in Dutch business account
- [ ] Bank statements showing ‚Ç¨25,000 startup equity
- [ ] 2-year income/revenue projections (certified by bookkeeper/accountant)
- [ ] Opening balance sheet
- [ ] Proof of sufficient funds for living expenses

**Supporting:**
- [ ] Diplomas, certificates, qualifications
- [ ] Reference letters (from clients, partners)
- [ ] CV / resume with entrepreneurial experience
- [ ] Proof of Dutch health insurance (or intent to obtain)

**All documents must be certified and translated** into Dutch, English, French, or German.

### Application Process

**Step 1: Prepare Business Plan + Evidence**
- Write comprehensive business plan addressing all RVO scoring criteria
- Gather supporting evidence (contracts, financials, references)

**Step 2: Apply for MVV + Residence Permit**
- If applying from abroad (which Otto will be): Apply at Dutch embassy/consulate
- In Brazil: Dutch Consulate-General in **S√£o Paulo**
- Submit IND form 7558 + all documents
- Provide biometrics (fingerprints, photo) at appointment

**Step 3: RVO Assessment**
- Embassy forwards application to IND ‚Üí IND sends to RVO
- RVO evaluates business plan against points system
- RVO advises IND (up to 3 months)

**Step 4: IND Decision**
- Processing time: **90 days** (extendable to 6 months)
- If approved: MVV visa sticker placed in passport (valid ~90 days)
- Must enter NL within MVV validity

**Step 5: Arrive in Netherlands**
- Collect biometric residence permit at IND desk within 2 weeks
- Register with KvK within 6 weeks (needs BSN)
- Register at municipality (get BSN number)
- Get Dutch health insurance

### Can You Apply from Brazil?
**YES.** Apply at the Dutch Consulate-General in S√£o Paulo. The process:
1. Book appointment online at consulate
2. Submit documents + biometrics
3. Consulate forwards to IND
4. Wait for decision (90 days to 6 months)
5. If approved, collect MVV sticker from consulate (within 3 months of approval)
6. Travel to Netherlands within MVV validity

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** You have only **3 months** from IND approval to collect MVV at consulate. Plan accordingly.

### Do You Need a Sponsor?
For self-employed: Generally **no recognized sponsor needed** (unlike employee permits). Your own business can serve as the basis. However, having a Dutch accountant/lawyer submit on your behalf is strongly recommended.

### Permit Duration
- Initial: **2 years** (some sources say 1 year)
- Renewable if: positive cash flow, ‚Ç¨4,500 capital intact, income ‚â•‚Ç¨1,657/month
- After 5 years: eligible for permanent residence

## 2. Schengen Exit Strategy

### The Problem
**As of January 1, 2025, ALL countries bordering the Netherlands by land are Schengen members.** Romania, Bulgaria, and Croatia all joined full Schengen (including land borders) by January 2025.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Border Considerations
- **Schengen exit:** When leaving the Schengen zone (entering Serbia/Bosnia), passport WILL be stamped. This documents the exit.
- **If Otto has overstayed Schengen 90/180 rule:** The exit stamp will show this. Could result in:
  - Fine at border

  - **This needs legal advice BEFORE leaving**
- **Vehicle:** Need Green Card insurance for non-Schengen Balkans

### üö® CRITICAL: Overstay Risk
If Otto is currently in NL without proper status, leaving Schengen will expose this at the border. He should:
1. Consult an immigration lawyer IN the Netherlands first
2. Understand the consequences of the exit stamp showing overstay
3. Consider whether voluntary departure with a lawyer's help could mitigate penalties
4. An overstay record could HURT the entrepreneur visa application

### Does Oopuo / Otto's Existing Companies Count?

**Yes, but structure matters:**

1. **If Oopuo is the Dutch entity:** Its revenue, clients, and track record directly support the application. This is ideal.

2. **If Oopuo is a foreign company:** Otto could register a Dutch branch (KvK) or create a new Dutch BV that contracts with Oopuo. The Dutch entity needs to show Dutch economic benefit.

3. **Recommended structure:**
   - Register a **Dutch BV** (or eenmanszaak)
   - This entity contracts with existing clients or Oopuo entities
   - Revenue flows through Dutch entity
   - Shows Dutch economic activity
   - Accountant certifies projections based on existing business relationships

4. **Key for IND:** They want to see that the business benefits the **Dutch economy** ‚Äî not just a shell. Evidence of:
   - Dutch clients or Dutch market activity
   - Local hiring potential
   - Innovation or unique expertise
   - Existing contracts/revenue

### Business Plan Requirements

The business plan is THE most critical document. It must address all RVO scoring categories:

**Section 1: Personal Experience (Category A)**
- Educational background and qualifications
- Previous entrepreneurial experience
- Industry expertise and track record
- Connections to the Netherlands (clients, partners, networks)
- Why Netherlands specifically

**Section 2: Business Viability (Category B)**
- Executive summary
- Market analysis (Dutch/EU market)
- Products/services description
- Revenue model
- 2-year financial projections (accountant-certified)
- Marketing strategy
- Competitive analysis

**Section 3: Added Value to Dutch Economy (Category C)**
- Innovation (what's new/unique)
- Job creation plans
