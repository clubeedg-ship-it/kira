## 1. Framework for SaaS/Hardware Hybrid Projections {#1-framework}

### Why Hybrid Is Different
ZenithCred has two revenue streams with very different profiles:
- **Hardware (light panels + biofeedback devices):** Lumpy, upfront revenue; higher COGS; inventory risk
- **SaaS/Platform (gamification tokens, dashboard, analytics):** Recurring revenue; high gross margin; churn risk

**The golden rule:** Model them separately, then consolidate.

### Recommended Model Structure (5 Sheets)

| Sheet | Contents |
|-------|----------|
| **Assumptions** | All key drivers in one place (pricing, growth rates, churn, headcount, CAC). Toggle scenarios here. |
| **Revenue** | Hardware units sold Ã— ASP + SaaS MRR buildup (new customers âˆ’ churned Ã— ARPU). Monthly Y1â€“Y2, quarterly Y3â€“Y5. |
| **Expenses** | COGS (hardware BOM, shipping, fulfillment) + OpEx (salaries, marketing, R&D, G&A). |
| **P&L / Cash Flow** | Consolidated income statement + cash flow. Show burn rate and runway explicitly. |
| **Dashboard** | Summary KPIs, charts, scenario toggle (base / bull / bear). Investor-facing. |

### Bottom-Up Approach (Seed-Appropriate)
At seed stage, top-down TAM-to-revenue is unconvincing. Instead:
1. **Start with sales capacity:** How many demos/month can you run? What's the close rate?
2. **Derive customers:** Demos Ã— close rate = new customers/month
3. **Derive revenue:** Customers Ã— (hardware ASP + monthly SaaS fee)
4. **Layer in churn and expansion** from Month 6+
5. **Validate against comparable growth rates** (see Section 3)

### Scenario Analysis
Build three scenarios sharing the same structure but different assumptions:

| Scenario | Y1 Revenue | Y3 Revenue | Key Difference |
|----------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Bear** | 60% of base | 50% of base | Slower sales cycle, higher churn |

| **Bull** | 140% of base | 180% of base | Faster adoption, lower churn, upsell |
