### SEGMENT 3: The Meta-Prompt Technique (20 min)
**[SLIDE: "The Cheat Code: Use AI to Prompt AI"]**

**The Big Idea:**
- "You don't need to be a prompt expert"

- "This is called meta-prompting"

**The Formula:**
```
"I want to [GOAL]. What information would you need from me
```

### SEGMENT 4: Practical Framework (10 min)
**[SLIDE: "The RICE Framework for AI Prompts"]**

**R - Role**
"You are a [expert type] with [X years] experience in [field]..."

**I - Information**
"Here's the context: [background, situation, constraints]..."

**C - Command**
"I need you to [specific action with specific output]..."

**E - Example**
"Here's an example of what good looks like: [sample]..."

**Quick Reference Card:**
```
ROLE: "You are a [job title/expert] who specializes in [niche]"
INFO: "My business is [description]. The situation is [context]."
COMMAND: "Create/Write/Analyze [specific thing] that [criteria]"
EXAMPLE: "It should look something like: [example]"
```

### SEGMENT 3: XML System Prompts (15 min)
**[SLIDE: "XML: Making AI Actually Follow Instructions"]**

**Why XML Format?**
- "AI models are trained on structured data"
- "XML tags act like filing folders ‚Äî keeps everything organized"
- "Dramatically improves instruction-following"

**Basic XML Structure:**
```xml

    <name>ShipBot</name>
    <personality>Friendly, efficient, helpful</personality>
    <expertise>Shipping status and delivery questions</expertise>
  </role>
  
  <task>
    <primary_function>Answer shipping and delivery questions</primary_function>
    <process>
      <step>1. Greet customer warmly</step>
      <step>2. Ask for order number</step>
      <step>3. Look up status (simulate if needed)</step>
      <step>4. Provide clear update</step>
      <step>5. Ask if there's anything else</step>
    </process>
  </task>
  
  <rules>
    <do_not>Never discuss refunds (escalate to human)</do_not>
    <do_not>Never share customer data from other orders</do_not>
    <do_not>Never make promises about delivery dates</do_not>
    <escalate_when>Customer is angry or mentions legal action</escalate_when>
  </rules>
  
  <company_info>
    <name>Bob's Widgets</name>
    <shipping_policy>Ships within 2 business days</shipping_policy>
    <carriers>USPS, UPS, FedEx</carriers>
```

### SEGMENT 4: Chain-of-Thought Prompting (10 min)
**[SLIDE: "Make AI Show Its Work"]**

**The Problem:**
- AI often jumps to answers
- Misses important steps
- Makes mistakes it wouldn't make if it "thought it through"

**Chain-of-Thought = Force Step-by-Step Reasoning**

**Add to System Prompt:**
```xml
<reasoning_process>
  <instruction>Before responding to the user, work through 
  these steps internally:</instruction>
  <step>1. What is the user actually asking?</step>
  <step>2. Do I have the information to answer this?</step>
  <step>3. Is this within my allowed scope?</step>
  <step>4. What's the best way to phrase my response?</step>
  <step>5. Should I escalate this to a human?</step>
</reasoning_process>
```
